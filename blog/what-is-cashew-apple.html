What is Cashew Apple? The Forgotten Tropical Fruit

By Mawun Valley Team · February 5, 2026

← Back to Field Notes

Taste it yourself: Join our Cashew Apple Jam Cooking Class and experience this unique fruit firsthand.

Everyone knows the cashew nut. But how many people have tasted the fruit it grows from? Meet the cashew apple — arguably the world's most wasted tropical fruit.

The Fruit Behind the Nut

Here's the surprising truth: the part we call a "cashew nut" is actually a seed. The cashew apple is the real fruit — a colorful, aromatic, nutritious fruit that 95% of the world has never tasted.

Why? Because cashew apples are incredibly perishable. Within 24 hours of falling from the tree, they begin to ferment. This makes commercial export impossible — and means most of the world's cashew apple harvest (millions of tons annually) simply rots on the ground.

What Does Cashew Apple Taste Like?

The flavor is unique and complex:

  • Sweet and tangy — like a mix of mango, citrus, and bell pepper
  • Astringent — a slight drying sensation in the mouth
  • Aromatic — tropical and fragrant
  • Juicy — extremely high water content

It's an acquired taste for some, but lovers of unusual tropical fruits often become obsessed.

Nutritional Powerhouse

The cashew apple is remarkably nutritious — containing 5 times more Vitamin C than an orange. It's also rich in:

  • Vitamin C (exceptionally high)
  • Iron
  • Phosphorus
  • Calcium
  • Antioxidants

In countries where it's consumed (Brazil, India, parts of Africa), cashew apple is valued as a health food.

Why Most People Have Never Tried It

Three reasons:

  1. Extreme perishability — can't be shipped fresh
  2. Focus on the nut — the export industry cares only about cashew nuts
  3. Geographic limitation — you must be where cashews grow during harvest season

This makes tasting fresh cashew apple a rare experience — available only to those who visit tropical cashew-growing regions during harvest.

How Cashew Apple is Used

In places where it's not wasted, cashew apple becomes:

  • Fresh juice — highly refreshing, consumed same day
  • Feni (India) — a distilled alcoholic spirit
  • Jam — our specialty at Mawun Valley Farm
  • Vinegar — used in cooking
  • Animal feed — in some areas
